Hey, my name is Amelia Untiedt

I am a GIS specialist who focuses on finding the hidden connections in the world. The natural world has always been a sanctuary of peace for me. As a child, I spent many hours running through forests and fields, exploring in ponds and rivers. Questions and fun facts about the environment occupied my mind at all times. I even conducted rudimentary fields experiments, from testing the best design for catching field mice to raising monarch catepilars to butterflies. Soon my passion and curiousty of the outdoors began to drive my career.
During my undergraduate experience at Gustavus Adolphus college, I began to study sociology and anthropology. During my studies, I began to realize the importance of including humanity into the natural sciences. I observed how many times, the impact of extensive footprint of the human world was not considered into models and research. I realized that many issues that may seem exclusively human, such as food scarcity, still have strong implications on the environment. I incorporated this realization into my work in order to develop well-rounded and sustainable research that benefits both the environment and humankind.
